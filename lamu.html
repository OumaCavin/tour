About Lamu Island

Lamu Island, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is Kenya's oldest and most beautifully preserved Swahili settlement, dating back over 1,300 years.

Located off the northern coast of Kenya in the Indian Ocean, Lamu Island is a treasure trove of Swahili architecture, culture, and history. The island's maze-like streets, coral stone buildings, carved wooden doors, and peaceful atmosphere create a unique time-warp experience where ancient traditions blend seamlessly with modern comfort.

With no cars, only donkeys and boats for transport, Lamu offers a peaceful retreat from modern life. The island is renowned for its beautiful beaches, traditional dhow sailing, rich handicrafts, and warm hospitality that reflects the generosity of Swahili culture.

Cultural Attractions

Discover Lamu's rich Swahili heritage and traditions

Lamu Town

Wander through the narrow streets of Lamu Old Town, a perfectly preserved 14th-century Swahili settlement with coral stone buildings and intricate wooden doors.

Swahili Architecture

Explore the magnificent Swahili architecture, from the Lamu Fort to numerous historic mosques, featuring distinctive Arab-influenced design and carved wooden elements.

Cultural Festivals

Experience traditional festivals like the Lamu Cultural Festival, featuring traditional dances, poetry, and dhow races that celebrate Swahili heritage.

Handicrafts

Discover local handicrafts including intricately carved doors, jewelry, textiles, and the famous Lamu furniture made from local hardwoods.

Swahili Cuisine

Sample authentic Swahili cuisine including fresh seafood, pilau, chapatis, and the famous "samaki wa kukaanga" (fried fish) prepared with local spices.

Living Heritage

Witness living Swahili culture where ancient traditions are still practiced daily, from Islamic prayers to traditional boat building techniques.

Beaches & Activities

Relax and explore Lamu's natural beauty and water activities

Pristine Beaches

Enjoy unlimited stretches of white sand beaches perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and relaxation away from crowds.

Dhow Sailing

Experience traditional dhow sailing trips to nearby islands, featuring sunset cruises and fishing expeditions with local crews.

Water Sports

Enjoy various water activities including snorkeling, diving, kayaking, and deep-sea fishing in the rich marine environment.

Island Exploration

Explore Lamu and neighboring islands like Manda Island by donkey cart, the traditional mode of transport on the island.

How to Get to Lamu

Multiple ways to reach this beautiful island paradise

By Air

Fly to Lamu Airport (LAU) from Nairobi (1.5 hours) or Mombasa (45 minutes). Direct flights available daily with Kenya Airways and Safarilink Aviation.

Nairobi to Lamu: KSh 15,000 - 25,000

By Sea

Take the traditional dhow or speedboat from Mombasa (4-6 hours by dhow, 2 hours by speedboat). Scenic journey along the Kenyan coast.

Traditional dhow experience included in some packages

By Road + Ferry

Drive to Mombasa then take the ferry to Likoni and continue to the coast. More adventurous route with scenic stops along the way.

Car rental and organized road trips available
