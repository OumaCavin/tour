About Tsavo National Park

Tsavo National Park is Kenya's largest national park, divided into Tsavo East and Tsavo West by the Nairobi-Mombasa highway. It spans over 21,000 square kilometers of diverse landscapes.

Famous for its "red elephants" that roll in red dust, Tsavo is one of Kenya's premier wildlife destinations. The park's unique red earth comes from the iron-rich soil, giving the elephants their distinctive rusty color. Tsavo is home to large herds of elephants, along with lions, cheetahs, giraffes, and numerous other wildlife species.

The park offers diverse landscapes from savanna grasslands to volcanic hills, river forests to desert-like stretches. The famous Mzima Springs provides crystal-clear water in an oasis setting, while Lugard Falls offers spectacular water features. Tsavo represents the essence of classic African safari experiences.

Tsavo East vs Tsavo West

Discover the unique features of each section of the park

Tsavo East

Classic Safari

Description: The larger eastern section of Tsavo National Park, offering vast open plains and classic African safari landscapes.

Key Features:
  • Vast open plains and savanna grasslands
  • Large concentrations of red elephants
  • Panoramic views across the Athi River
  • Mudanda Rock with panoramic views
  • Yatta Plateau, one of the world's longest lava flows
  • Historical sites and ancient geological formations
Activities:
Game Drives Bird Watching Photography Hiking

Tsavo West

Diverse Landscapes

Description: The western section featuring more diverse landscapes including mountains, springs, and volcanic formations.

Key Features:
  • Mzima Springs with crystal-clear underwater viewing
  • Volcanic landscapes and crater views
  • Lugard Falls with dramatic water features
  • Chaimu Peak with stunning views
  • Poacher's Lookout offering panoramic vistas
  • Lake Jipe with rich birdlife
Activities:
Game Drives Swimming Hiking Underwater Viewing

Tsavo Wildlife

Discover the incredible animals that inhabit this vast park

Red Elephants

Tsavo's famous elephants that wallow in red earth, creating their distinctive rusty color and unique appearance.

Big Five

All members of the Big Five can be found in Tsavo, including large prides of lions and healthy populations of buffalo.

Antelopes

Large herds of various antelope species including impala, gazelle, and eland roaming the open plains.

Birdlife

Over 500 bird species including bustards, hornbills, secretary birds, and various species of eagles.

Major Attractions

Don't miss these iconic Tsavo landmarks and features

Mzima Springs

Natural springs producing 250 million liters of crystal-clear water daily, with underwater viewing hide and lush vegetation.

Underwater Viewing Fresh Water Nature Walk

Lugard Falls

Dramatic waterfalls and rapids formed by the Galana River, creating spectacular natural water features.

Mudanda Rock
