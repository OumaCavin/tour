About Amboseli National Park

Located in southern Kenya near the Tanzanian border, Amboseli National Park is renowned for its spectacular views of Mount Kilimanjaro and its large herds of African elephants.

Known as the "Land of Giants," Amboseli is famous for having some of the largest elephants in Africa. The park's name comes from a Maasai word meaning "springs of water," reflecting the presence of seasonal swamps and lakes that attract diverse wildlife throughout the year.

The park offers excellent opportunities to see the Big Five, with particularly impressive elephant populations. The backdrop of Mount Kilimanjaro creates one of Africa's most iconic safari landscapes, providing stunning photographic opportunities.

Park Highlights

  • Large elephant herds with iconic tuskers
  • Panoramic views of Mount Kilimanjaro
  • Observation Hill for 360° park views
  • Swamps and lakes attracting diverse wildlife
  • Excellent bird-watching opportunities
  • Maasai culture and traditions

Wildlife in Amboseli

Discover the incredible animals that call Amboseli home

African Elephants

Home to some of the largest elephant herds in Africa, with bull elephants having impressive tusks. The park's elephants are known for their large size and are often seen wallowing in the mud.

Big Five

Spot lions, leopards, elephants, buffalo, and rhinoceros in their natural habitat. The park's open grasslands make wildlife viewing exceptional.

Bird Species

Over 400 bird species including pelicans, kingfishers, hornbills, and various species of eagles and vultures that soar over the park.
