Lake Naivasha: Kenya's Freshwater Paradise

Lake Naivasha is the largest freshwater lake in Kenya and the only natural freshwater lake in the Great Rift Valley. This stunning lake offers unique wildlife experiences and breathtaking scenery.

The lake is famous for its large population of hippos, which can be observed from the safety of boats or viewing points. Surrounded by acacia woodlands and the dramatic backdrop of the Rift Valley, Lake Naivasha provides a tranquil escape and incredible biodiversity.

Why Visit Lake Naivasha?

  • Close encounters with hippos in their natural habitat
  • Exceptional bird watching with over 400 species
  • Stunning views of the Great Rift Valley
  • Boat safaris on pristine freshwater
  • Peaceful retreats and luxury accommodations

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about Lake Naivasha

Swimming is not recommended in Lake Naivasha due to hippos and crocodiles. The lake is best enjoyed through boat safaris and viewing from designated areas. The water is safe for drinking when properly treated.

Boat safaris offer close encounters with hippo families, exceptional bird watching including fish eagles and pelicans, and beautiful lake scenery. You may also see waterbucks, giraffes, and various waterfowl.

Crescent Island is accessible by boat from the lake shore. Guided walks are organized where you can safely walk among wildlife including giraffes, zebras, antelope, and various bird species. Walking safaris are one of the unique experiences at Lake Naivasha.

Lake Naivasha is Kenya's only natural freshwater lake in the Great Rift Valley. It offers unique experiences like walking safaris on Crescent Island, close hippo encounters, and exceptional bird watching in a stunning natural setting.
